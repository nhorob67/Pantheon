# Schedules & Briefings

Schedules let you automate recurring messages from your assistant. The main feature is the **daily briefing** -- a configurable morning summary posted to your Discord channel covering weather, grain bids, and scale ticket activity.

## Daily briefing

The briefing is a single scheduled message that combines multiple sections. Configure it from **Settings > Briefings**.

### Briefing sections

Toggle each section on or off:

| Section | What it includes |
|---|---|
| **Weather Forecast** | Temperature, wind, precipitation, spray windows |
| **Grain Bids** | Cash bids from configured elevators, basis changes |
| **Scale Ticket Summary** | Yesterday's deliveries, bushels by crop, load counts |

### Configuration

| Setting | Options |
|---|---|
| **Enable/disable** | Toggle the entire briefing on or off |
| **Send time** | Time of day (e.g., 06:30) |
| **Timezone** | America/Chicago, America/Denver, America/New\_York, America/Los\_Angeles |
| **Discord channel** | The channel ID where the briefing is posted |

<Callout variant="info" title="Finding your channel ID">
To get a Discord channel ID: enable Developer Mode in Discord (User Settings > Advanced), then right-click the channel and click "Copy Channel ID".
</Callout>

## Per-agent cron jobs

In addition to the briefing system, individual agents can have their own scheduled jobs:

| Schedule | Default Time | Required Skill | What it posts |
|---|---|---|---|
| Morning Weather | 6:00 AM daily | Weather | Full morning briefing with forecast, alerts, and spray window assessment |
| Daily Grain Bids | 9:00 AM Mon--Fri | Grain Bids | Cash grain bids from all configured elevators |

Configure per-agent cron jobs from **Settings > Agents** on each agent's card. Times are in your farm's local timezone.

## Cost considerations

Each scheduled run consumes API tokens:

- Morning weather briefing: ~1,500--3,000 tokens
- Daily grain bids: ~2,000--4,000 tokens
- Full briefing (all sections): ~3,000--6,000 tokens

Factor this into your [spending cap](/docs/billing/spending-cap) and usage estimates. Disabling unused schedules is the easiest way to reduce costs.
