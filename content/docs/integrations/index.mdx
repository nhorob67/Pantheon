# Integrations

Integrations let your AI agents connect to external services like Google Sheets, Gmail, Google Calendar, Slack, and hundreds more. Instead of being limited to skills and local tools, your agents can read spreadsheets, send emails, create calendar events, and interact with the services you already use.

Pantheon integrations are powered by [Composio](https://composio.dev), a platform that provides secure, authenticated access to 800+ third-party APIs through a single connection. Composio runs as a remote MCP server alongside your built-in tools.

## How it works

When you enable integrations, Pantheon:

1. Creates a Composio entity for your account (no separate Composio sign-up required).
2. Provisions a remote MCP server that your agents can call.
3. Adds the server to your workspace configuration alongside your existing MCP servers.

From that point, your agents have access to the actions exposed by your selected toolkits. You authorize each service individually through a standard OAuth flow -- the same "Sign in with Google" experience you use elsewhere.

```
Your Workspace
├── Built-in Platform Tools
│   ├── memory
│   ├── knowledge-files
│   ├── email
│   └── custom-skills
└── Remote MCP Servers
    └── composio (SSE)
        ├── Google Sheets actions
        ├── Gmail actions
        ├── Google Calendar actions
        └── ...selected toolkit actions
```

## Key concepts

### Toolkits

A toolkit is a bundle of related actions for a single service. For example, the **Google Sheets** toolkit includes actions for reading spreadsheets, writing data, and creating new sheets. You select which toolkits your agents have access to -- only selected toolkits are available.

Pantheon curates a set of toolkits with descriptions tailored to common use cases. See [Available Toolkits](/docs/integrations/toolkits) for the full list.

### Connections

A connection is an authorized link between your agents and a specific service. When you connect Google Sheets, for example, you go through Google's OAuth consent screen and grant your agents permission to access your spreadsheets. The connection is managed by Composio -- your Google credentials are never stored on Pantheon servers.

See [Connecting Services](/docs/integrations/connecting-services) for the full OAuth walkthrough.

### Remote MCP servers

Composio runs as a remote MCP server over Server-Sent Events (SSE). Your agents connect to Composio's hosted endpoint, which routes requests to the appropriate service API.

Composio runs entirely on Composio's infrastructure.

<Callout variant="info" title="No extra cost for Composio">
Composio integration is included in your Pantheon subscription. The Composio API key is platform-level -- you do not need your own Composio account or API key.
</Callout>

## What can your agents do with integrations?

Here are some examples of what teams can ask once integrations are connected:

| Service | Example prompt | What happens |
|---|---|---|
| Google Sheets | "Add today's metrics to the tracking spreadsheet" | Reads your data, writes rows to your Google Sheet |
| Google Sheets | "Pull the budget numbers from the planning sheet" | Reads specific cells/ranges from a spreadsheet |
| Gmail | "Email the project summary to the client" | Composes and sends an email with relevant data |
| Google Calendar | "Schedule the design review for next Tuesday at 2 PM" | Creates a calendar event with details |
| Google Calendar | "What's on the team calendar this week?" | Lists upcoming events |
| Slack | "Post today's summary to the #updates channel" | Sends a message to a Slack channel |
| Notion | "Save this meeting notes summary to Notion" | Creates a page in your Notion workspace |

<Callout variant="tip" title="Integrations work alongside skills">
Integrations complement your existing skills. Your agents can combine data from custom skills with integration actions (write to spreadsheet, send email, create calendar event) in a single response.
</Callout>

## Getting started

1. Go to **Settings > Integrations** in your Pantheon dashboard.
2. Toggle the **Composio** switch to enable the integration.
3. Select the toolkits you want your agents to access.
4. Click **Save Toolkits** to apply changes.
5. Connect each service by clicking **Connect** and completing the OAuth flow.

For detailed setup instructions, see [Setup Guide](/docs/integrations/setup).

## Privacy and security

- Your service credentials (Google tokens, Slack tokens, etc.) are encrypted with AES-256-GCM and managed by Composio. They are never stored on Pantheon servers.
- Composio acts as a secure proxy -- your agents access services through authorized API calls only.
- Disconnecting a service immediately revokes all access and removes stored tokens.
- You control exactly which toolkits your agents can use. Disabling a toolkit removes the agents' ability to call those actions.

See [Security and Privacy](/docs/integrations/security) for full details.
