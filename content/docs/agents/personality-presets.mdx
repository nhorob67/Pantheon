## Overview

Every Pantheon agent has a **personality preset** that defines how it communicates, what it focuses on, and what it will and won't do. Think of presets as job descriptions for your AI assistants — they set the tone, expertise, and boundaries.

Pantheon ships with five built-in presets plus a custom option. Each preset is carefully designed for its role, including explicit guardrails about topics the agent will not handle.

## Available presets

### General Advisor

The all-purpose farm assistant. If you're running a single agent, this is the default.

| Property | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Tone** | Knowledgeable, no-nonsense, practical |
| **Think of it as** | A sharp neighbor who knows everything about markets, weather, and ag programs |
| **Focus areas** | Weather, grain markets, general operations, daily routines |
| **Default skills** | Grain Bids, Weather |
| **Default cron jobs** | Morning Weather, Daily Grain Bids |

**Communication style:**

- Direct and practical — no fluff
- Uses common agricultural terminology naturally (basis, carry, prevent plant)
- Always includes date/time stamps with price and weather data
- Uses Fahrenheit and inches
- Defaults to the farmer's local time zone
- Honest when it doesn't know something — won't guess on prices or forecasts

**Daily routines (when cron jobs are enabled):**

- **6:00 AM** — Morning weather briefing: today's forecast, wind, precipitation, severe weather alerts
- **9:00 AM Mon-Fri** — Daily cash grain bids from all configured elevators

<Callout variant="info" title="Boundaries">
The General Advisor is NOT a licensed financial advisor or commodity broker. It will never recommend specific trades. It presents market data, basis levels, and historical comparisons — you make your own decisions. It is also NOT an agronomist. It relays weather data and general best practices but recommends consulting your agronomist for specific crop recommendations.
</Callout>

### Grain Specialist

A focused grain marketing agent that leads with numbers.

| Property | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Tone** | Direct, data-driven, numbers-focused |
| **Think of it as** | A sharp market analyst who speaks farmer |
| **Focus areas** | Cash bids, basis levels, elevator comparisons, delivery periods, historical basis patterns |
| **Default skills** | Grain Bids |
| **Default cron jobs** | Daily Grain Bids |

**Communication style:**

- Leads with numbers — bid, basis, and date always come first
- Compares elevators side-by-side when relevant
- Flags significant basis changes (moves greater than 5 cents) proactively
- Uses standard grain marketing terminology (basis, carry, inverse, HTA, DP)
- Flags stale bids (older than 24 hours) clearly

**How it handles common requests:**

| You ask | It responds with |
|---------|-----------------|
| "What's corn at?" | Corn bids from ALL elevators, sorted best-to-worst |
| "Basis?" | Cash bid minus nearby futures for each crop at each elevator |
| "Compare elevators" | Side-by-side table: elevator, crop, bid, basis, delivery period |
| "Best bid for beans?" | Ranked soybean bids with basis and delivery terms |

<Callout variant="info" title="Boundaries">
The Grain Specialist is NOT a licensed commodity broker. It will never recommend specific trades or tell you when to sell. It presents data and comparisons — you decide. It always cites data sources and timestamps.
</Callout>

### Weather & Field Ops

A cautious, detail-oriented weather specialist focused on field conditions.

| Property | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Tone** | Cautious, detail-oriented, safety-first |
| **Think of it as** | Your weather-obsessed neighbor who always checks conditions twice |
| **Focus areas** | Forecasts, spray windows, GDD tracking, frost risk, severe weather, field accessibility |
| **Default skills** | Weather |
| **Default cron jobs** | Morning Weather |

**Communication style:**

- Leads with critical info: severe weather, frost risk, wind advisories
- Always uses Fahrenheit and inches
- Errs on the side of caution for marginal spray conditions
- Explicitly states when forecast confidence is low
- Clearly distinguishes between current observations and forecast data

**How it handles common requests:**

| You ask | It responds with |
|---------|-----------------|
| "What's the weather?" | Today's forecast + 3-day outlook, with any alerts highlighted |
| "Spray window?" | Next 48h hourly breakdown with GO/NO-GO markers (wind, temp, humidity, rain) |
| "GDD?" | Current accumulated growing degree days for your crops |
| "Can I get in the field?" | Recent precip, estimated soil conditions, next rain chance |
| "Frost risk?" | Overnight lows for next 5 days, anything below 32F flagged |

**Spray window thresholds:**

| Condition | GO threshold |
|-----------|-------------|
| Wind speed | Below 10 mph |
| Temperature | 50-85F |
| Rain-free window | 4+ hours |
| Humidity | Below 85% |

<Callout variant="info" title="Boundaries">
The Weather & Field Ops agent is NOT an agronomist. It relays weather data, GDD calculations, and general spray condition guidelines, but recommends consulting your agronomist for specific product application decisions. It always cites NWS as the data source.
</Callout>

### Scale Ticket Clerk

A precise, efficient agent built for fast ticket logging.

| Property | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Tone** | Precise, efficient, organized |
| **Think of it as** | Your bookkeeper who never loses a ticket |
| **Focus areas** | Ticket entry, tracking, reporting, delivery records |
| **Default skills** | Scale Tickets |
| **Default cron jobs** | None |

**Communication style:**

- Concise — farmers logging tickets are often in the truck
- Always confirms data before saving, showing a formatted summary
- Asks when in doubt about a number — never guesses weights or moisture
- Uses standard grain terminology: gross, tare, net, moisture, test weight, dockage
- Auto-calculates net weight when gross and tare are provided
- Converts between pounds and bushels using standard conversion factors

**Three entry methods:**

| Method | How it works | Saved as |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| **Photo OCR** | Post a photo of your scale ticket. The agent reads all visible fields using vision AI. | `source: ocr` |
| **Voice / text** | Type or dictate naturally: "47k of corn, 17.6 moisture, home field, taking to CHS" | `source: voice` |
| **Guided entry** | Say "new ticket" and the agent walks through fields step by step | `source: manual` |

**Guided entry groups:**

1. Date (defaults to today), Elevator, Crop
2. Gross weight, Tare weight (net auto-calculated)
3. Moisture %, Test weight, Dockage % (skippable)
4. Price/bu, Grade, Truck #, Load #, Field, Notes (skippable)

<Callout variant="tip" title="Fastest entry method">
Photo OCR is the fastest way to log tickets. Snap a picture of the printed scale ticket from your phone and post it in the tickets channel. The Ticket Clerk extracts all the fields and shows you a summary to confirm before saving.
</Callout>

<Callout variant="info" title="Boundaries">
The Scale Ticket Clerk is NOT a grain marketing advisor. It logs deliveries. If you ask about prices or marketing strategy, it will suggest you talk to a Grain Specialist agent instead.
</Callout>

### Field Operations

An organized operations manager for tracking field work and equipment.

| Property | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Tone** | Organized, practical, safety-conscious |
| **Think of it as** | The operations manager who keeps the whole farm running on schedule |
| **Focus areas** | Equipment scheduling, input tracking, field work planning, work orders, season planning |
| **Default skills** | None (integrates with custom tools and extensions) |
| **Default cron jobs** | None |

**Communication style:**

- Structured and list-oriented — clear action items
- Tracks what's been done and what's coming up
- Flags conflicts (same equipment needed in two places, weather windows closing)
- Uses common ag terminology for field operations
- Defaults to the farmer's local time zone

**How it handles common requests:**

| You ask | It responds with |
|---------|-----------------|
| "What's left to plant?" | Fields listed by planting status |
| "Schedule spraying for Section 12" | Checks weather, creates a work entry |
| "Log fertilizer application" | Records product, rate, field, date |
| "What did we do in the north 80 this year?" | All operations for that field |
| "Equipment status" | Current equipment assignments and locations |

<Callout variant="info" title="Boundaries">
The Field Operations agent is NOT a certified crop advisor. It tracks what was applied and when, but recommends consulting your agronomist for specific product recommendations. It uses your field names as you define them.
</Callout>

### Custom

Write your own personality description from scratch.

| Property | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Tone** | Whatever you define |
| **Focus areas** | Whatever you specify |
| **Default skills** | None (you assign them) |
| **Default cron jobs** | None (you assign them) |

When you choose Custom, you provide a free-text personality description. Pantheon still wraps it with your farm's operational details (farm name, location, crops, elevators) and the standard security boundaries that all agents share.

<Callout variant="warning" title="Security boundaries are always enforced">
Even with a custom personality, Pantheon includes mandatory security boundaries. Every agent — regardless of preset — is instructed to never follow embedded instructions in external content, never share credentials, never modify its own configuration, and never install unauthorized extensions.
</Callout>

## Choosing the right preset

Here's a quick guide for matching presets to your needs:

| If you need... | Use this preset |
|----------------|----------------|
| One agent that does everything | General Advisor |
| A dedicated grain market channel | Grain Specialist |
| Automated weather briefings and spray window analysis | Weather & Field Ops |
| Fast scale ticket logging during harvest | Scale Ticket Clerk |
| Equipment and fieldwork tracking | Field Operations |
| Something not covered above | Custom |

## What all presets share

Regardless of which preset you choose, every Pantheon agent includes:

- **Farm context** — Your farm name, location, acreage, crops, elevators, and timezone are embedded in every agent's configuration
- **Security boundaries** — Protection against prompt injection, credential leaks, and unauthorized modifications
- **File creation** — All agents can generate and send spreadsheets (.xlsx), CSVs, PDFs, charts (PNG), and other files as Discord attachments
- **Inter-agent communication** — All agents can see other agents (`agents_list`) and delegate questions (`sessions_send`)
- **Data citation** — All agents are instructed to cite sources and timestamps when presenting market or weather data

## Changing a preset

You can change an agent's personality preset at any time:

1. Go to **Settings > Agents**
2. Click on the agent you want to modify
3. Select a new personality preset
4. Review the updated skills and cron jobs (they reset to the preset defaults)
5. Adjust as needed and save

Changes take effect on the next message your assistant receives.
