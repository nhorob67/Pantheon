## Overview

This guide walks you through the entire setup process. By the end, you'll have a working AI assistant in your Discord server that can fetch grain bids, check weather, and log scale tickets.

## Step 1: Create your account

Go to the FarmClaw signup page and enter your email address. We use **magic link authentication** — no password required. Check your inbox for the login link and click it to access your dashboard.

<Callout variant="tip" title="Check your spam folder">
Magic link emails occasionally land in spam or promotions folders. The email comes from FarmClaw and contains a one-time login link.
</Callout>

## Step 2: Activate your subscription

After logging in, you'll be prompted to activate your subscription:

- **$50/month** base subscription
- **Metered API usage** billed at cost (typically $5–15/month depending on usage)
- Billing is handled securely through Stripe

Click "Subscribe" to complete checkout. You'll be redirected back to FarmClaw to begin onboarding.

## Step 3: Complete the onboarding wizard

The onboarding wizard has four steps:

### Farm Profile

- **Farm name** — A display name for your operation
- **State** — Select your state (ND, SD, MN, MT, IA, or NE)
- **County** — Your county name
- **Primary crops** — Select from: Corn, Soybeans, Spring Wheat, Winter Wheat, Durum, Barley, Sunflowers, Canola, Dry Beans
- **Acres** — Total acres in your operation (up to 100,000)

### Elevator Setup

Add the grain elevators you deliver to. For each elevator:

- **Name** — e.g., "CHS Fargo"
- **Website URL** — The page where cash bids are posted
- **Crops** — Which commodities to track at this elevator

We provide presets for major elevator companies: CHS, ADM, Cargill, AGP, Columbia Grain, and Gavilon with state-specific defaults.

### Weather Location

- **Location** — Your town or zip code
- **Coordinates** — Latitude and longitude for precise NWS forecasts
- **Timezone** — Auto-populated based on your state

### Discord Connection

- **Bot token** — Your Discord bot token (see the [Discord Setup](/docs/discord/server-setup) guide for creating a bot)

## Step 4: Wait for provisioning

After completing the wizard, FarmClaw provisions your instance:

1. A cloud server is created for your instance
2. Your configuration is built from the onboarding data
3. The AI assistant container is deployed
4. Your Discord bot comes online

This process typically takes 2–3 minutes. You'll see a status indicator on your dashboard.

## Step 5: Send your first message

Once your instance shows "Running" status, open your Discord server and try one of these:

```
What are today's corn bids?
```

```
What's the weather forecast for this week?
```

```
Log a scale ticket
```

Your AI assistant will respond in the channel. That's it — you're up and running.
